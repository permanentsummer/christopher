Since 2021 · Algarve, Portugal

Short-Term Rentals

Two apartments. The first real business. The one that forced me to build everything else.

How it started

Moved to Portugal, saw the Algarve market was strong, acquired the first apartment. Everything that followed — the cleaning app, the automation setup, the pricing system — was built because of what broke first.

How it runs today

  • Two properties, Algarve region
  • Airbnb + Booking.com, synced via Make.com and Google Calendar
  • Cleaning coordination through SummerClean (the app I built)
  • WhatsApp alerts fire automatically on booking triggers
  • Trilingual guest communication — Portuguese, English, French
  • Pricing reviewed weekly, not set-and-forget

Why it matters beyond the income

Rental income is the base that makes everything else possible. But more than that — this business generated SummerClean as a by-product. Problems that needed to be solved became products. That pattern has repeated across the businesses.

The thing I kept learning

A well-run average property beats a poorly-run premium one. The asset matters less than the system around it. Build the system first.

Since 2022 · B2B · Europe

Permanent Summer
Import/Export

UV protection nets and safety flooring. The business nobody asks about until they need exactly that.

What it is

B2B distribution of AUSTRONET 204 UV protection nets (agricultural markets) and safety flooring for playgrounds and outdoor installations. No storefront. All relationships. Sales cycles are long, contracts are real.

The operation

  • Sourced from Austrian manufacturing partners
  • Clients across Portugal, France, broader EU
  • Agricultural buyers, playground contractors, landscape architects
  • Quotes coordinated in English, French, and Portuguese
  • All supply relationships direct — no intermediaries

Why B2B only, always

Consumer retail needs infrastructure that would dilute everything. B2B means fewer, bigger relationships. Each one matters more. That's where I want to spend the energy.

What took the longest to understand

In B2B, the relationship is the product. You can have the best net on the market — if the client doesn't trust your delivery, they won't buy. The first six months were almost entirely about earning trust with no track record. Nobody tells you how long that actually takes.

Since 2022 · France & Mauritius

Tapni

NFC digital business cards. Started as Head of Sales. Now franchise owner for France and Mauritius.

What Tapni is

Tap a card against any smartphone — your full contact profile, links, social accounts transfer instantly. No app required. The product is real and the category is early, which is exactly when it's interesting.

The transition

Joined early as Head of Sales. Built B2B pipelines from scratch for a product most buyers had never seen. Then acquired the franchise for France and Mauritius — two very different markets that require two different approaches.

  • France: enterprise B2B, corporate networking culture, conference distribution
  • Mauritius: relationship-driven, existing local network, personal credibility
  • Franchise model: territorial exclusivity, operational independence

What's different as an owner

As Head of Sales the goal was moving product. As franchise owner the goal is building a sustainable local operation. Different problem set entirely. The mindset shift took longer than the contract paperwork.

The thing that transfers to everything

Selling something nobody's heard of is a skill. Every new business has this problem — you know the value, the buyer doesn't yet. The answer is always the same: one tap converts better than a ten-slide deck. Make the demo obvious.

Since 2024 · cleaning.permanentsummer.co

SummerClean

Built to solve a real problem I had. Then other people had the same problem.

The problem

Coordinating cleaning across rental properties is messier than it sounds. WhatsApp groups don't scale. Shared calendars don't alert anyone. A cleaner shows up to the wrong apartment, a checkout gets missed — these aren't hypothetical problems.

How it's built

Single-file HTML/CSS/JS. Pulls from Google Sheets via CSV. Make.com handles the automation layer — iCal imports from Airbnb and Booking.com sync through Google Calendar into the data source.

  • PIN-protected admin mode — cleaner view vs manager view
  • WhatsApp alerts via CallMeBot on booking triggers
  • List view + calendar view
  • Trilingual: Portuguese, English, French
  • Fully mobile-optimised

How it became a product

Two other operators adopted it without me pitching it. That was the signal. Cleaned up the code, added documentation, made it available as a standalone tool.

Version 1 had too many features. Stripped it back to the core workflow and adoption tripled among cleaning staff. Classic lesson, learned the hard way.

What I keep thinking about

Build for the real user, not the imagined one. The cleaning staff needed one screen, one clear piece of information. Not a dashboard. A checklist. Knowing the difference is the whole product job.

Since 2023 · E-commerce · DTC

Cœur Salé

Organic cotton clothing brand. Everything in-house. A different kind of project from everything else.

Where it came from

The Algarve has a strong aesthetic — salt, sun, coast, nothing rushed. Cœur Salé ("salty heart") was built around that. Organic cotton wasn't a marketing decision; it was a product quality one. Cheaper materials produce cheaper experiences.

What "in-house" actually means

  • Product design and sourcing
  • Photography — location-shot, all original
  • AI-powered embroidery personalisation system
  • Packaging design
  • Website (GitHub Pages + Cloudflare)
  • Customer communication, fulfilment, post-purchase

The early-stage cost of doing it all yourself is high in time. The result is a brand with actual coherence.

What didn't work

Ran paid ads before the buyer profile was clear. Burned budget. Paused, gathered feedback, rebuilt from word-of-mouth. Also spent a week debugging a Cloudflare CDN issue that was corrupting HTML files on deploy. Unglamorous. Solved it with atomic file writes in Python.

The honest version

E-commerce before customer insight is expensive guesswork. Know why the first ten customers bought before spending on the next thousand. Word-of-mouth retention beats paid traffic retention every time.
